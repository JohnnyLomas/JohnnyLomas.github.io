Highlighted Projects

Transgenic: Using generative AI for genome annotation

I have developed and trained a seq2seq transformer for translating raw DNA sequence into a text-based protein-coding gene annotation. I am currently working to benchmark the model's performance and prepare a manuscript detailing my approach. Check back soon for updates!

Sylvan: Genome annotation pipeline

I developed and benchmarked Sylvan, which is a comprehensive genome annotation pipeline that uses multiple evidence sources to compute a genome annotation from a raw genome assembly. After computing a draft annotation, Sylvan uses a novel semi-supervised filtration algorithim to increase precision by removing spurious gene models. The pipeline is implemented in Snakemake to facilitate distributed computing via SLURM and is containerized with Singularity to facilatate installation and deployment. A manuscript describing the pipeline and benchmarking it's performance is under review. The source code is available on GitHub

Applied Genome Annotation

As a PhD student at the University of Nevada, Reno, I participated in the assembly and annotation of the Ixodes scapularis (tick), Mesembryanthemum crystallinum (ice plant), and Opuntia cochenillifera (cactus) genomes. I used bioinformatics analyses to uncover the genomic underpinnings of key traits. In ticks, we focused on olfactory receptors while in the two plants we looked at the operation of CAM photosynthesis and other mechanisms of drought resistance. Publications for the plant species are in review or in preparation.

Publications

  1. Tang, Haibao, Vivek Krishnakumar, Xiaofei Zeng, Zhougeng Xu, Adam Taranto, Johnathan S. Lomas, Yixing Zhang et al. "JCVI: A versatile toolkit for comparative genomics analysis." Imeta 3, no. 4 (2024): e211.

    A bioinformatics software toolkit for comparative genomics analysis. This package is widely used for its implementation of MCScan, a synteny analysis and visualization tool important for identifying structually similar regions between two genome assemblies. I contributed code to the gene feature format (GFF) analysis tools in the JCVI package.

  2. Tang, Haibao, Wenqian Kong, Pheonah Nabukalu, Johnathan S. Lomas, Michel Moser, Jisen Zhang, Mengwei Jiang, Xingtan Zhang, Andrew H. Paterson, and Won Cheol Yim. "GRABSEEDS: extraction of plant organ traits through image analysis." Plant Methods 20, no. 1 (2024): 140.

    An image processing tool which aids in the collection of seed trait data.

  3. Alaasam, Valentina J., Cassandra Hui, Johnathan S. Lomas, Stephen M. Ferguson, Yong Zhang, Won Cheol Yim, and Jenny Q. Ouyang. "What happens when the lights are left on? Transcriptomic and phenotypic habituation to light pollution." Iscience 27, no. 2 (2024).

    An analysis of the behavioral and transcriptomic response of birds to nighttime light stimulus as a model for the impacts of light pollution. I processed and analyzed RNA sequencing data using repeated response experimental design to identify metabolic pathways which were perturbed by various light stimuli.

  4. Nuss, Andrew B., Johnathan S. Lomas, Jeremiah B. Reyes, Omar Garcia-Cruz, Wenlong Lei, Arvind Sharma, Michael N. Pham et al. "The highly improved genome of Ixodes scapularis with X and Y pseudochromosomes." Life science alliance 6, no. 12 (2023).

    A high-quality genome assembly and annotation of the tick species Ixodes scapularis. I used RNA sequencing data to assess the performance of the assembly and annotation for RNA-seq read mapping and transcript assignment. Also used R (ggplot) to create publication quality figures for the manuscript.
